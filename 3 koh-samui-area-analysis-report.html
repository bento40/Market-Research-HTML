Samui's 6 Accommodation Zones

According to the C9 Hotelworks 2025 Market Review, Samui's 634 registered properties and 24,188 keys distribute across 6 primary areas. The breakdown by share of total supply is:

Hotel Supply Distribution by Area — 2024 (% of Keys)
Area Popularity Trend — Past vs Future (Estimated Share)

Chaweng dominates with 20% of total keys — the island's commercial heart with its 7km beach, shopping (Central Samui), nightlife, and the densest restaurant scene. Bophut follows at 15%, anchored by Fisherman's Village and increasingly attracting digital nomads and boutique hotel investment. Lamai holds 14% and is quietly transforming upmarket. Choeng Mon at 13% is the luxury enclave (Ritz-Carlton, Meliá, SALA, Cape Fahn). Maenam at 8% serves the quiet family/couple market. The remaining 30% ("Others") covers Taling Ngam, Bang Por, Lipa Noi, Nathon, and hillside/interior properties.

Trend 2020→2030: Bophut and "Others" (west/southwest coast) are gaining share as new branded supply enters outside the traditional Chaweng/Lamai corridor. All 4 pipeline hotels are located in Bophut, Lamai, or Taling Ngam — none in Chaweng, suggesting the island's growth axis is shifting.

Past (2019): Dominated by young backpackers and party groups (~35%), with couples (~25%) and budget-conscious first-timers. Chaweng was synonymous with nightlife.

Future (2030): Expect couples and families to reach ~55% combined as budget stock declines and mid-range properties reclassify upward. The young backpacker share will continue shrinking as Chaweng matures. Digital nomad presence grows with improved co-working infrastructure.

Average ADR by Area

ADR varies dramatically by area — a direct reflection of the accommodation mix and positioning of each zone. Island-wide ADR grew 9% in 2024, with April 2025 posting a remarkable 21% YoY surge.

Estimated ADR Range by Area — 2024/2025 (USD per night)

Choeng Mon commands the highest ADR thanks to the luxury brand concentration (Ritz-Carlton, Cape Fahn). Chaweng has the widest ADR spread — from $40 budget rooms to $500+ suites — reflecting its full-spectrum market. Bophut sits in the premium-midrange sweet spot. Maenam offers the best value on the north coast.

Pricing Intelligence (Trip.com 2025/2026 data): Average hotel price in Koh Samui peaks in January (~$211/night) and is cheapest in April (~$159/night). 3-star hotel deals start at ~$31/night; 4-star from ~$112; 5-star from ~$180. Chaweng Beach averages $323/night across all segments on Trip.com.

Hotel Segment Growth — Past & Future

The trajectory is clear: Samui's hotel market is moving structurally upmarket. Every pipeline hotel is Luxury or Upper Upscale. No new Midscale or Upper Midscale branded supply is entering. Meanwhile, aging budget stock is converting, rebranding, or closing — shrinking the lower tiers as a share of total supply.

Segment-by-Segment Growth Analysis

Luxury (34% → projected 37% by 2030)

Past (2019): ~28% of tracked supply. Anchored by legacy icons — Four Seasons, Banyan Tree, Conrad, Six Senses, InterContinental. Post-pandemic renovation wave upgraded several properties.

Present (2024): Grew to 34% as properties like Marasca Samui (2024, boutique luxury on Chaweng) opened and existing Upper Upscale properties repositioned upward. White Lotus effect supercharged demand, with Four Seasons ADR jumping 40%+.

Future (2030): Nivata by Hilton (2025), SO/ Sofitel (2026), and Fivelements (2026) all enter at Luxury/Upper Upscale tier. Projected to reach ~37% of supply. Growth is supply-driven (new builds) and demand-driven (wellness travelers, set-jetters, European long-haul).

Upper Upscale (28% → projected 30% by 2030)

Past (2019): ~24% of tracked supply. Major brands included W Koh Samui, Meliá, Centara Reserve, Kimpton Kitalay.

Present (2024): Grew to 28% through rebranding — NH Collection (formerly Peace Resort, est. 1977) is the signature example of a Midscale property jumping 2 tiers via brand affiliation. SAii's adult-only conversion (Dec 2024) also repositioned upward.

Future (2030): Wyndham Garden (533 keys, 2028) enters at the Upper Upscale/Upscale boundary — the single largest addition to any tier. Potential Hyatt Unbound Collection in Chaweng Noi would also land here. The rebranding wave will continue as independent properties seek chain affiliation to access loyalty program guests and reduce OTA dependence.

Upscale (12% → stable ~12% by 2030)

Past (2019): ~13% of supply. Boutique and independent resorts in the $100–200 range.

Present (2024): Slight compression to 12% as properties either upgraded to Upper Upscale (via rebranding) or were squeezed down by competition. Centara Lifestyle Lamai (2024, 61 keys) entered at this tier.

Future (2030): This tier acts as the "transition zone" — properties are either climbing into Upper Upscale via brand affiliation or sliding down as they age. Relatively stable in share but high churn. Holiday Inn Express (if confirmed, Bophut) would anchor the upper end of this tier.

Upper Midscale (19% → projected 15% by 2030) — CBR's Current Tier

Past (2019): ~22% of supply. The traditional "3-star beachfront" tier. Properties like Chaweng Buri, Chaweng Garden, Samui Paradise, and many independent family-run resorts along Chaweng and Lamai.

Present (2024): Down to 19%. No new branded supply entering this tier. The shrinkage comes from two directions: (1) properties upgrading to Upscale/Upper Upscale via renovation + rebranding (the path CBR is on), and (2) some aging properties declining into Midscale or exiting the market.

Future (2030): Projected to decline to ~15% as the upgrade cycle continues. This is precisely why CBR's 4-star reclassification is strategically urgent — the Upper Midscale tier is shrinking, meaning properties that stay here face increasing commoditization and OTA price compression, while those that step up into Upscale/Upper Upscale access better ADR and a more resilient customer base.

Midscale (7% → projected 6% by 2030)

Past (2019): ~13% of supply. Budget bungalows, basic guesthouses, and backpacker lodges concentrated in Chaweng, Lamai, and Maenam.

Present (2024): Dropped sharply to 7%. Many budget properties closed during COVID (36% of hospitality businesses closed in Q2 2021). Survivors face rising land costs, labor shortages, and competition from Airbnb. COSI Samui and Lub d represent the "new Midscale" — branded, design-forward budget options replacing traditional bungalows.

Future (2030): Will continue to contract to ~6%. Traditional budget bungalow stock is essentially a dying category on Samui — replaced either by branded budget concepts (COSI model) or Airbnb/vacation rentals. Budget travelers will increasingly shift to Koh Phangan and Koh Tao for better value.

The Big Picture: Samui is undergoing a one-way structural shift from a 5-tier pyramid to a top-heavy diamond — Luxury and Upper Upscale together will represent ~67% of tracked supply by 2030 (up from ~52% in 2019). The middle and bottom are compressing. Every property must decide: move up, or get squeezed. CBR's wellness rebrand + 4-star reclassification is the right move at the right time.

Average Prices, Occupancy & Popular Hotels

Island-wide occupancy averaged 75–85% in FY2023, with 2024 posting a 12% YoY increase. January 2025 peaked with an 8% YoY gain. ADR grew 9% in 2024, and April 2025 surged 21% YoY — the strongest monthly ADR growth on record.

Monthly Average ADR — 2022 vs 2023 vs 2024 vs 2025 (USD)

The ADR curve follows Samui's distinct seasonality: peak season (Dec–Feb) driven by European winter escapes, a shoulder (Mar–Apr) as Songkran brings a domestic bump, a low season dip (May–Jun, Sep–Oct) during Gulf monsoon, and a recovery (Jul–Aug) from summer holiday travelers. The key takeaway: each successive year's curve sits higher than the last — Samui has consistently ratcheted up pricing power.

Monthly Estimated Occupancy — 2022 vs 2023 vs 2024 vs 2025 (%)

Occupancy follows a similar seasonal pattern but with two notable features: (1) the trough is rising — low-season occupancy has improved from ~45% (2022) to ~58% (2024), driven by wellness year-round visitors, digital nomads, and cruise arrivals, and (2) January consistently leads, with 2025 posting the strongest January on record (+8% YoY).

How Hotels Compete on Samui

Hotel competition on Samui operates across 8 primary dimensions. The competitive playbook has shifted dramatically post-pandemic, with wellness, direct booking, and "Instagrammability" rising as differentiators.

Competitive Differentiation Strategies — Ranked by Adoption (2024–2025)

The 8 Competitive Battlegrounds

1. Private Pool Villas (90% of upper-tier properties) — The single most important product differentiator. Nearly every new-build or renovation includes pool villas. SAii converted to adult-only pool villas in Dec 2024. CBR's 44 pool villas are a core competitive asset.

2. OTA Ranking & Review Scores (critical for mid-range) — 80% of travelers read reviews before booking. The difference between Booking.com 7.5 and 8.5 is material. Hotels invest in review response workflows, guest satisfaction tracking, and targeted renovation to move scores.

3. Wellness & Spa Programming (fastest-growing) — Moving from "spa as amenity" to "wellness as brand identity." Kamalaya pioneered this; Fivelements (2026) doubles down. Centara Reserve, Ritz-Carlton, and Garrya Tongsai Bay all repositioning around wellness. 130% higher spend from wellness guests.

4. F&B / Breakfast Quality (highest review impact) — Your review analysis confirmed breakfast is the most visible F&B touchpoint. Hotels compete on buffet breadth, local ingredient sourcing, beachfront dining settings, and Instagrammable presentations.

5. Brand Affiliation & Loyalty Programs — Hilton, Marriott (Ritz-Carlton, W), IHG (Kimpton, InterContinental), Accor (SO/ Sofitel) and Minor (NH Collection, Anantara) all expanding on Samui. Loyalty program members book direct, reducing OTA commission. Independent properties must compete harder on uniqueness.

6. Instagram / Social Media Appeal — 40% of bookings influenced by Instagram/TikTok. Hotels invest in photogenic infinity pools, drone-worthy angles, floating breakfasts, and "set-jetting" packages tied to White Lotus. The SAii "Sleep Well" concept, W Koh Samui's social scene, and Four Seasons' coconut-grove aesthetics are all social-first strategies.

7. Sustainability & Eco-Credentials — Increasingly important for European travelers (56% of arrivals). Six Senses leads in sustainability. GSTC certification, solar power, composting, reef restoration, and plastic-free operations are becoming table-stakes for premium positioning.

8. Experience Curation & Day Passes — The "villa + day pass" model is emerging: guests stay in private Airbnb villas but buy day passes ($45–100) at luxury resorts for pool, spa, and F&B. Hotels monetize non-guests while villas capture accommodation spend. This hybrid model redefines competition.

CBR Competitive Position: You compete in dimension #1 (44 pool villas ✓), #2 (OTA optimization — your top priority), #3 (wellness rebrand — in progress), and #4 (breakfast quality — identified gap from review analysis). Dimensions #5–8 represent stretch opportunities. The OTA reclassification to 4-star is the single highest-leverage competitive move because it shifts you into a higher visibility tier on Booking.com where the 35% upper-midscale traveler segment browses.
